### 3. Vocabulary Deep Dive

- **关西腔** (Guānxī qiāng)
  - **IPA:** /kæn.ˈsaɪ ˈdaɪ.ə.lekt/
  - **Cultural Context:** Often associated with comedy, boisterousness, or friendliness in Japanese media. In English translation, it's often rendered as a "Southern accent" or "Brooklyn accent" to maintain the "rough but hearty" feel.
  - **Example:** He spoke with a **thick Kansai dialect**. (他操着一口浓厚的关西腔。)

- **剔骨/剥离** (Bōlí)
  - **IPA:** /diː.ˈtætʃ/ / /ˈflieɪ/
  - **Context:** To peel away or flay. Used here to describe the digital deconstruction of her avatar.
  - **Mermaid Graph:**

    ```mermaid
    stateDiagram-v2
    [*] --> HumanForm
    HumanForm --> Deconstruction : Firewall Breach
    Deconstruction --> DataStream : "Razors/Flaying"
    DataStream --> Reconstruction : Code Recompilation
    Reconstruction --> DigimonForm : Lilamon/Rosemon
    ```

- **荆棘** (Jīngjí)
  - **IPA:** /ˈθɔːrnz/ / /ˈbraɪ.əz/
  - **Meaning:** Thorns/Brambles. Iconic to Rosemon’s design.
  - **Metaphor:** Constraint and power. The thorns "tightening" suggests the physical pressure of the Mega-level data.
  - **Example:** She was ensnared in **thorns and brambles**. (她被荆棘丛所困。)

- **措手不及** (Cuò shǒu bù jí)
  - **IPA:** /kɔːt ɒf ɡɑːrd/
  - **Etymology:** To not have time to move one's hand.
  - **Meaning:** Caught off guard; taken by surprise.
  - **Example:** The sudden rainstorm **caught me off guard**. (骤雨让我措手不及。)
