
"That explains it," Owl said, snapping his fingers to indicate. "A subspecies of Gabumon; there's no part of his body that needs metal filling except for the horn on his head. Let's make a hypothesis: supposing he's just an ordinary newcomer with decent popularity in the guild. Even if his horn broke one day, he wouldn't immediately rush to find us and entrust us to take him to the Hephaestus Workshop to find Master Changsun. He could ask anyone in the guild and know where it is..."

"Makes sense."

Miss Jade sat up straight and picked up on Owl's point. Holding her wine glass, she laid out her deduction: "What if, and I'm just saying what if, that newcomer named Solomon is Plutomon's man?"

"Then we're back to square one," Captain Agnes answered her bluntly. "Nothing has changed."

"So, everything only makes sense if he's working for the true beneficiary."

Xiao Yong didn't quite understand and asked Jade, who was sitting beside him: "How so?"

"Think about it," Miss Jade offered us a new train of thought, "What kind of role did he play in this matter? He was an errand-runner, someone who could get Free Birds involved in the Bird-Beast Struggle, participate in it, and antagonize the Mediation Organization. That is to say, even though he was just a pawn in the beneficiary's hands, the position he occupied was extremely important. With him, this whole scheme could succeed."

For a moment, everyone fell into contemplation, and the atmosphere grew inexplicably quiet. Boss Adam walked past us holding two empty wine glasses. Seeing us all engaged in such serious thought, he couldn't help but join in. The moment he opened his mouth, he deeply shocked us.

"Listening to so much from you all... if you ask me, it's pretty obvious that Jack from the Nether Club did this."

I hurriedly stopped his words and asked, "Wait, Boss, how can you be so certain?"

"That's easy; I heard him talk about it before." He couldn't help but sigh, "You guys basically arrived in Midgard a few days late, so you missed all those guild conflicts and personal grudges between Guild Masters. That guy has always been at odds with Wind. Strictly speaking, during that time, apart from me, the remaining nine Guild Masters basically couldn't stand each other. They often bickered over trivial matters, and made a fuss over things big and small... Don't mind me being blunt, but none of those nine are easy to deal with, and none of them are good people."

Hearing this, Xiao Yong's face looked slightly awkward: "You're... really seeing through everything."

"Ultra is the same, paranoid to a certain extent." Boss Adam inevitably rubbed his forehead, his expression not looking very pretty: "In the very beginning, he was the first to have a falling out with Wind. I remember the most serious time: the two of them just fought each other directly in their human forms. I never expected that a grown man would lower himself to argue with a woman. I couldn't pull them apart no matter how hard I tried, and the most critical part was that the punches landing on her face were really heavy, holding back not a single bit of power. The most outrageous part is that this conflict didn't just happen once or twice. One time, it even broke out right here..."

"Then you..."

"I dealt with both of them and threw them out. I told them if they dared to come back here, I'd deal with them every time they showed up."

Xiao Yong raised an eyebrow and muttered casually: "No wonder the Guild Master sent me here to take his place. Turns out he was afraid of getting beaten."

With the Boss's words, we could basically lock onto the other suspect for this incident, Jack. About him, we only knew he was highly "mysterious", and nothing else. And it wasn't just us, the regular customers in the bar were the same.

"Take my advice," Boss Adam rarely showed a serious look. "Don't go looking for him, let alone bring him up in public places casually. His spies are all over Midgard. Once you offend him, you'll definitely be in for it, unless you're purposely looking to die."

As the Guild Master's sister, Miss Jade asked the question on our behalf: "Is he... that strong?"

"More than strong, the key is he's very dangerous, no matter in which world." After uttering this meaningful sentence, Boss Adam snapped his fingers, signaling the bartender to send another round of drinks to our table.

"Brother, why haven't I heard you mention him before?"

"At that time, you were all still very weak and hadn't grown up yet, so naturally, you wouldn't go provoking dangerous people or getting involved with dangerous things, especially when lives were on the line."

Speaking of this, Boss Adam coughed twice, actively changing the subject: "Voltobautamon Jack. With your current strength, you're not strong enough to be his opponents. You must know that Plutomon being instantly killed by him was not entirely based on luck, but more so on his own terrifying strength."

Boss Adam suggested that if we still wanted to know the truth behind the matter, we could try finding the Nether Club's prodigy, **X**. He's like the other self of the Nether Club; there's nothing he doesn't know.

Agnes had some concerns about this: "I've heard people talk about that guy... his temper isn't too great, and his face looks quite scary. Going to him and beating around the bush might not be that easy."

"No need to be afraid." Boss Adam gave us more advice. "His weakness is known by all of us Guild Masters. Back in the day, Master Muca taught every junior who offended him upon their first meeting with restraint, only stopping when necessary. However, X was an exception. He was dealt with so miserably that it left him with deep psychological trauma, which he couldn't forget for years. So, if you feel his attitude towards you isn't particularly friendly during your interaction with him, you can try using the 'Ba-ye' name to intimidate him."

"But Brother, didn't Master Muca pass away long ago?"

"Yes, but that guy still feels that 'Ba-ye' lives on in his heart, always ready to whip him."

After everyone's deliberation, we unanimously decided that the follow-up part of this matter would continue to be handed over to Miss Agnes, who volunteered, along with the **Nordic 5** she led, for an in-depth investigation. Miss Jade originally wanted to join in too. First, she was stopped by her older brother, Boss Adam. Second, she also had to busy herself with the affairs of the **UN P5 Raiders**. According to her description, among the five of them, she was the only one who had any free time.

"What about the other four?"

"**Dragon** and **Zero** have run off to who-knows-where, **Lana** has to accompany her family's Poseidon, **Mante** is busy rushing reports. So it's just your poor sister acting as five people, doing work that doesn't belong to her, and being ordered around by the director in various ways."

Owl appropriately interjected: "If you feel you can't handle it alone, you can rely on us a little bit."

"You guys?" Miss Jade showed some contempt towards Owl. "Stop joking. The only truly 'free' people left in Free Birds are just you two. I don't want to be that third wheel. If you have that kind of time, help my brother sort out those people who always owe him and refuse to pay their tabs, and take better care of him for me."

"We aren't babysitters."

"Just treat it as my commission." Miss Jade said, glancing at her brother beside her and gently bumping him: "The bar is resting for a week starting next week. Someone is going to have an operation, and I've already contacted a doctor and a bed for him. Once he goes, just drop my name directly. After a while, when I'm done with the Raiders' business, I'll bring a basket of flowers to visit him."

The brother-sister bond between them was truly enviable.

After parting ways, Owl went to visit the remaining three wounded members of Free Birds. I stayed at the bar, helping the Boss clean up the empty glasses on the table and handling the aftermath.

"Oh right, Boss, I had to use the **Seed of Promotion** you gave me before. I know this item is very precious and rare, so I'll consider it as owing you. In the future, if I have the chance, I will definitely..."

He believed that the strong didn't need such things, so he consigned it with me, saying that in the future, if I also became a strong person like him and no longer needed this thing, I should give it away to someone who truly needed it."
