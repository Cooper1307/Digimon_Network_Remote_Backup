# Chapter 15: Beautiful Ending (第十五章 美丽的终结)

The matter reached a perfect resolution.

After returning, we cross-checked information with Boss Adam's sister, Jade, and the members of the **Nordic Five Raiders**. We roughly understood the cause and effect of this "Beast-Bird Struggle."

"There are still parts of this we haven't fully figured out, but that doesn't matter."

These words were problematic, sounding as if those people died in vain. As for how to let their souls find peace, Miss Agnes probably never thought about it.

A group of people sat in the bar around two tables and began to speculate about the motive of the mastermind Plutomon. No need to worry about leaking the news; the arrival of the mediation organization had already caused a significant loss of customers for the bar. It was fortunate that Guild Master Adam was an exception among Guild Masters; since **The 'Drink' Bar** was established, he had always maintained a neutral state with other guilds and clearly marked his relationships, so he didn't have to face the mediation organization and cross swords with them.

At this point, Owl pulled me aside and asked about my previous situation. I knew he was worried about me, and at the same time, I clearly knew that the situation at that time—even thinking about it now afterward—would still cause me deep fear.

At that time, Mervamon's great sword was about to hit my head. In a moment of desperation, I fell back onto the grass. Before her **Final Strike Roll** could reach me, I swallowed the gift Boss Adam gave me—a **Seed of Promotion**. Subsequently, the red on my body gradually turned to white, and the cloak on my back was dyed with a touch of pink, adding a bit of girlishness to the original "Queen of Flowers and Grass," thus breaking through my limits.

**Rosemon** — **Burst Evolution** — **Rosemon: Burst Mode**.

The Burst Evolution at the critical moment was something I'll never forget in my life.

"And then?"
"Then I discovered a brand-new operation mode."

I said before that Bandai Sakura has never liked fighting or killing. Thus, the network data fulfilled her wish, weakening a part of her combat ability and thereby deepening her charm attribute. For example, giving the opponent a sudden kiss, making them confused and even a bit shy; while she was stunned, amplifying the aura of the cloak, wrapping her in gently, making her fall into a deep sleep.

"So you didn't destroy her?"
"Right."
I didn't deny it.

"Then... where is she?"
"Still in the old place," I explained to Owl. "She was sleeping too soundly, and I couldn't drag her. To be honest, I didn't expect the 'Charm' effect to be so strong. I'll tell you secretly, don't tell the others—I slapped her twice, quite hard, and even that didn't wake her up."

After hearing this, Owl shrugged helplessly and stated: "Well, as long as she's alive."

I didn't know which of us two he was talking about.

Through this battle, the mediation organization successively lost four major generals: Junomon, Ceresmon, Apollomon, and Dianamon. Guild Master Wind of **Wind-Chasing Brigade** and Wing of **Rainbow Starlight** also died honorably. Now **Wind-Chasing Brigade** is under the care of the surviving Plume. Xiao Yong barely defeated the holding-back Master Changsun, and the battle between Guild Master Ultra and Marsmon also ended in a draw, so at least they didn't drag down our "All-Victory Team."

By contrast, the situation on the **Free Birds** side was a bit tragic. Crane was beaten to pieces, her armor and feathers damaged to various degrees, requiring a long time to recover. Dove and Falcon, naturally, didn't need much explanation; both were defeated by their respective opponents and lost convincingly.

"Truly? I don't believe it."
"Believe it or not, it's a fact." Owl told me. "They are all our own people. Usually leave them some face, and don't mention this again in front of them—people need space to grow."

I strongly agreed with his last sentence; I think so too.

"So, this matter is just over like this?"
"Of course not." Owl was very sure about this. "It can only be considered a comma at most; there are still too many things waiting for us to discover and understand. No matter who the ultimate beneficiary is, we at least need to know the reason why they did it."

"Is pure revenge a possibility?"
"Until the truth is clear, everything is possible."

Thus, this mission drew to a rounded comma. From everyone's contemplative posture, it was as if there was still something on the table that we had ignored, or rather, had never noticed, thinking its existence was reasonable.

"Um, let me ask a nosy question." Reminding people of what they've overlooked is part of my job as the support role. "Did any of you ever look into that newcomer who first came to us with the request?"

Everyone present shook their heads—no one knew that newcomer. At the same time, Owl stood up, slapped his forehead, and hit upon a sudden realization: "Right, we haven't determined his identity yet... we all fell into a misunderstanding before, thinking the culprit was Plutomon, but what if he wasn't?"

"Besides, we still don't know what his Digimon image is," I continued to add from the side.

As a representative of **Beast King's Fangs**, Xiao Yong, after hearing our introduction, roughly had an idea in his mind and could basically determine the identity of that candidate. He then introduced him to us: "That guy is indeed a newcomer, named Solomon. His digital image is a **Psychemon**. He's one of the newcomers who is relatively close to the Master, often running errands for him. He has good popularity in the guild, and we all like him."

"That explains it," Owl said, snapping his fingers to indicate. "A subspecies of Gabumon; there's no part of his body that needs metal filling except for the horn on his head. Let's make a hypothesis: supposing he's just an ordinary newcomer with decent popularity in the guild. Even if his horn broke one day, he wouldn't immediately rush to find us and entrust us to take him to the Hephaestus Workshop to find Master Changsun. He could ask anyone in the guild and know where it is..."

"Makes sense."

Miss Jade sat up straight and picked up on Owl's point. Holding her wine glass, she laid out her deduction: "What if, and I'm just saying what if, that newcomer named Solomon is Plutomon's man?"

"Then we're back to square one," Captain Agnes answered her bluntly. "Nothing has changed."

"So, everything only makes sense if he's working for the true beneficiary."

Xiao Yong didn't quite understand and asked Jade, who was sitting beside him: "How so?"

"Think about it," Miss Jade offered us a new train of thought, "What kind of role did he play in this matter? He was an errand-runner, someone who could get Free Birds involved in the Bird-Beast Struggle, participate in it, and antagonize the Mediation Organization. That is to say, even though he was just a pawn in the beneficiary's hands, the position he occupied was extremely important. With him, this whole scheme could succeed."

For a moment, everyone fell into contemplation, and the atmosphere grew inexplicably quiet. Boss Adam walked past us holding two empty wine glasses. Seeing us all engaged in such serious thought, he couldn't help but join in. The moment he opened his mouth, he deeply shocked us.

"Listening to so much from you all... if you ask me, it's pretty obvious that Jack from the Nether Club did this."

I hurriedly stopped his words and asked, "Wait, Boss, how can you be so certain?"

"That's easy; I heard him talk about it before." He couldn't help but sigh, "You guys basically arrived in Midgard a few days late, so you missed all those guild conflicts and personal grudges between Guild Masters. That guy has always been at odds with Wind. Strictly speaking, during that time, apart from me, the remaining nine Guild Masters basically couldn't stand each other. They often bickered over trivial matters, and made a fuss over things big and small... Don't mind me being blunt, but none of those nine are easy to deal with, and none of them are good people."

Hearing this, Xiao Yong's face looked slightly awkward: "You're... really seeing through everything."

"Ultra is the same, paranoid to a certain extent." Boss Adam inevitably rubbed his forehead, his expression not looking very pretty: "In the very beginning, he was the first to have a falling out with Wind. I remember the most serious time: the two of them just fought each other directly in their human forms. I never expected that a grown man would lower himself to argue with a woman. I couldn't pull them apart no matter how hard I tried, and the most critical part was that the punches landing on her face were really heavy, holding back not a single bit of power. The most outrageous part is that this conflict didn't just happen once or twice. One time, it even broke out right here..."

"Then you..."

"I dealt with both of them and threw them out. I told them if they dared to come back here, I'd deal with them every time they showed up."

Xiao Yong raised an eyebrow and muttered casually: "No wonder the Guild Master sent me here to take his place. Turns out he was afraid of getting beaten."

With the Boss's words, we could basically lock onto the other suspect for this incident, Jack. About him, we only knew he was highly "mysterious", and nothing else. And it wasn't just us, the regular customers in the bar were the same.

"Take my advice," Boss Adam rarely showed a serious look. "Don't go looking for him, let alone bring him up in public places casually. His spies are all over Midgard. Once you offend him, you'll definitely be in for it, unless you're purposely looking to die."

As the Guild Master's sister, Miss Jade asked the question on our behalf: "Is he... that strong?"

"More than strong, the key is he's very dangerous, no matter in which world." After uttering this meaningful sentence, Boss Adam snapped his fingers, signaling the bartender to send another round of drinks to our table.

"Brother, why haven't I heard you mention him before?"

"At that time, you were all still very weak and hadn't grown up yet, so naturally, you wouldn't go provoking dangerous people or getting involved with dangerous things, especially when lives were on the line."

At that point, Boss Adam coughed twice and deliberately changed the subject. "Voltobautamon Jack. With your current strength, you're still no match for him. You should know that when he killed Plutomon in an instant, it wasn't just luck. More than anything, it was his own terrifying strength."

Boss Adam suggested that if we still wanted to know the truth behind the matter, we could try finding the Nether Club's prodigy, **X**. He's like their version of me in the Nether Club, the kind of man who seems to know everything.

Agnes had some concerns about this: "I've heard people talk about that guy... his temper isn't too great, and his face looks quite scary. Going to him and beating around the bush might not be that easy."

"No need to be afraid." Boss Adam gave us more advice. "His weakness is known by all of us Guild Masters. Back in the day, Master Muca taught every junior who offended him upon their first meeting by always pulling his punches. However, X was an exception. He was dealt with so miserably that it left him with deep psychological trauma, which he couldn't forget for years. So, if you feel his attitude towards you isn't particularly friendly during your interaction with him, you can try using the 'Ba-ye' name to intimidate him."

"But Brother, didn't Master Muca pass away long ago?"

"Yes, but that guy still feels that 'Ba-ye' lives on in his heart, always ready to whip him."

After everyone's deliberation, we unanimously decided that the follow-up part of this matter would continue to be handed over to Miss Agnes, who volunteered, along with the **Nordic Five Raiders** she led, for an in-depth investigation. Miss Jade originally wanted to join in too. First, she was stopped by her older brother, Boss Adam. Second, she also had to busy herself with the affairs of the **UN P5 Raiders**. According to her description, among the five of them, she was the only one who had any free time.

"What about the other four?"

"**Dragon** and **Zero** have run off to who-knows-where, **Lana** has to accompany her family's Poseidon, **Mante** is busy rushing reports. So it's just your poor sister acting as five people, doing work that doesn't belong to her, and being ordered around by the director in various ways."

Owl appropriately interjected: "If you feel you can't handle it alone, you can rely on us a little bit."

"You guys?" Miss Jade showed some contempt towards Owl. "Stop joking. The only truly 'free' people left in Free Birds are just you two. I don't want to be that third wheel. If you have that kind of time, help my brother sort out those people who always owe him and refuse to pay their tabs, and take better care of him for me."

"We aren't babysitters."

"Just treat it as my commission." Miss Jade said, glancing at her brother beside her and gently bumping him. "The bar's closing for a week starting next week. Someone has to undergo surgery, and I've already arranged both the doctor and the hospital bed for him. Once he gets there, he can just give my name. After a while, when I'm done with the Raiders' business, I'll bring a basket of flowers to visit him."

The brother-sister bond between them was truly enviable.

After parting ways, Owl went to visit the remaining three wounded members of Free Birds. I stayed at the bar, helping the Boss clean up the empty glasses on the table and handling the aftermath.

"Oh right, Boss, I had to use the **Seed of Promotion** you gave me before. I know this item is very precious and rare, so I'll consider it as owing you. In the future, if I have the chance, I will definitely..."

"No need to return it."

"Eh?" I was certain I hadn't misheard.

"Strictly speaking, that one wasn't mine," Boss Adam said. "Back then, when the **Seed of Promotion** was first designed, Master Muca accidentally obtained one. He believed that the strong didn't need such things, so he consigned it with me, saying that in the future, if I also became a strong person like him and no longer needed this thing, I should give it away to someone who truly needed it."

It was treated like a sort of token, passed down through the hands of two seniors, finally arriving with me.

"Boss, about the 'Master Muca' you mentioned, I asked Owl many times before, but he wouldn't tell me. Could you tell me the story about him?"

"Of course. I see you've almost finished cleaning up. Shall we chat while we drink?"

"Sure."

"Be my guest."
