# Chapter 12: Battle of Sanhara (第十二章 鏖战)

At the moment of judgment, the "Claws of Sanction" bore down upon us, and our ten bodies immediately reacted.

Falcon was still leading the charge, taking the initiative to spin his body and unleash "Beast Wolf Great Revolution," successfully deflecting Merukimon's "Thousand Fist." Before his opponent could draw his beloved saber "Aztec" to tear open space and summon otherworldly beasts, Falcon preemptively locked blades with him using his "Yellow-Beast Crescent-Blade." From that moment, their duel shifted from a contest of speed to a clash of pure strength.

Dove followed closely behind, relying on his massive frame. He grappled his opponent, Bacchusmon, dragging him into the nearby mountains before unleashing a relentless barrage of heavy bombardments. The sole reason he opted to spar with the "Brewmaster of the Digital World" was because his mechanized body was completely immune to poison. Shouldering the burdens his teammates could not overcome on their own—this was his absolute code of conduct as their [Defense Position].

Crane faced off against Jupitermon's wife, Junomon. As soon as they clashed, they soared into the heavens and vanished from everyone's sight. In fact, it wasn't just Crane; Guild Master Wind and the duo from "Rainbow Starlight" did the exact same. The only difference was that their battles would weave through the clouds, occasionally piercing the veil of the clouds to let the sunlight through, or diving deep into the dark clouds, leaving behind fleeting trails of beauty for those below to catch a glimpse of.

The most intense spectacle, however, was undeniably the duel between the beasts.

Facing off against Marsmon—his own evolutionary form—Guild Master Ultra showed not a single ounce of fear. Even fully aware that his odds of victory were less than ten percent, he never retreated a single step. He maintained the fiery spirit of a veteran mentor, setting a sterling example for his junior, Xiao Yong. A flurry of fists and kicks erupted as they exchanged complex martial arts sequences. Aside from being slightly outmatched in raw strength, Guild Master Ultra held his ground perfectly in every other aspect—neither combatant willing to yield an inch.

"As expected of the Guild Master of *Beast King's Fangs*. Compared to the scum in Midgard, you truly operate on a different level."

"I'll say the same for you."

Even if the body takes a beating, the mouth must never surrender so easily. Biting back with equal ferocity was the duty of a true "brawler," and the foundational creed of a hero.

Capitalizing on the swirling dust storm to blind his foe, Guild Master Ultra pushed his fighting spirit to the absolute limit. He invoked "Burn Bancho Punch," channeling the roaring inferno from his entire body into his right fist, and blasted a massive, lion-shaped wave of pure flame at the enemy. Marsmon, however, was ready. He absorbed the searing gift head-on, wrapping the intense flames around his own body. Stomping hard against the earth, he launched himself out of the dust cloud toward the scorching sun within reach, preparing to execute his most magnificent finishing move from the sky.

"Prepare yourself!"

"My thoughts exactly!"

With his fighting spirit burning at the absolute max, Guild Master Ultra made no compromise in terms of sheer momentum. Staring down the enemy's execution stroke, the only proper response was to serve up an equally devastating ultimate skill.

"—Corona Sanctions!"

"Taste this—Flash Bancho Punch!"

The collision of two absolute forces at a single focal point unleashed a catastrophic shockwave. It tore the grass from its roots, shattered the bedrock beneath, and warped the once-gentle breeze into a devastating gale—incinerating everything combustible within line of sight into ash.

"Master—!"

"Take care of yourself first!"

Compared to a veteran steeped in rich combat experience, Xiao Yong—the "Rising Star"—was far from composed.

With no luxury to worry about his Guild Master, he found himself utterly suppressed by Master Changsun. He was battered relentlessly by eight metallic arms wielding an arsenal of heavy weapons. In the face of such a coordinated onslaught, the numerous drills mounted on his cyborg frame were effectively useless. Before we mobilized, I had actually asked Owl to gauge Master Changsun's combat prowess. According to Owl, the blacksmith wasn't considered an overwhelming threat in raw power, but whenever he decided to get rough, his sheer ferocity was overwhelmingly intimidating—easily terrifying opponents into a passive state of continuously taking a beating. Looking at the scene now, Owl couldn't have been more right.

Yet, no matter how green the youngster was, he absolutely refused to willingly become a mere punching bag for an old senior. Relying on his nimble stature, he attempted to weave through the strikes time and again. Though he took plenty of glancing blows in the process, he managed to keep the damage within tolerable limits, stubbornly preventing himself from being pinned down for too long.

"Forgive me, Senior!"

Leaning forward into a low roll, he narrowly evaded a lethal trifecta of sword, hammer, and staff swung by Master Changsun. As he tumbled, he spun up the bladed gauntlets on his arms. The very second he found his footing, he launched a devastating storm of rapid-fire strikes—"Gyro Smash"—straight into the rapidly approaching senior.

Absorbing the junior's furious barrage head-on, Master Changsun grunted in obvious pain. He dropped to one knee, letting out a heavy sigh to lament how terrifying the younger generation had become. Then, without warning, the old man fired on all cylinders, unleashing a torrential counter-offensive. His backmost arms, gripping heavy morning stars, led the charge. The moment Xiao Yong leapt backward to evade them, Changsun hurled two solid metal cudgels from his front arms, striking the boy dead center. As Xiao Yong threw his head back and howled in agony, the master's remaining pairs of swords and hammers swung into a brutal cross-formation—aiming directly for Xiao Yong's limbs and chest, threatening to pulverize and dismember the boy in one fell swoop.

"Don't blame me, kid. Heaven's will is hard to defy!"

Heaven's will? Did that mean the Mediators were actually being forced into this against their own volition?

The weapons were too fast, too close. Before Xiao Yong could even flinch, the dual hammers and twin blades were inches from caving in his armor. Resistance was futile—survival now demanded divine intervention. And that intervention came in the shape of Owl.

Having secretly condensed a "Dark Terra Destroyer" into the palm of his hand, Owl utilized the exact microscopic window he used to sidestep Jupitermon's "Punish Judge" to casually hurl the compressed orb straight toward his old friend. The blast spectacularly shattered the swords and hammers in Changsun's grip, saving the junior's life in the nick of time. Simultaneously, the blast served as a blunt warning from Owl: *There has never been such a thing as "Heaven's will" in Midgard. Even if there was, it was severed long ago by the twin "Kikurin" blades wielded by Senior "Muca." As his former colleague, you must never stain your hands with an act Muca would despise.*

Master Changsun easily read his old comrade's intent; the entire exchange was seamlessly natural to both of them.

When the smoke cleared, the tide had already turned. Capitalizing on the opening Owl had bought for him, Xiao Yong revved the blades on his forearms to maximum RPM. He generated the violent tornado of a "Qualialise Blast," instantaneously swallowing Master Changsun's massive figure.

"You've got the time to worry about others?"

"Why shouldn't I? I’m merely taking a page out of your book." Owl turned to meet Jupitermon's gaze head-on. "If you weren't constantly considering your own allies, terrified of catching them in the crossfire, you would have dropped your 'Wide-Range Divine Punishment' to completely wipe out a Virus-type seed like me ages ago. Am I wrong?"

"Sharp."

"You flatter me."

Without another word, Jupitermon thrust his arms wide, violently striking the heavy mallets in his hands together. He summoned a localized storm front above Owl, relentlessly raining down precision "Mabolt" strikes. Shielding himself against the downpour of lightning with his "Brave Shield," Owl blitzed toward Jupitermon. Once he closed the gap into the kill zone, he seized the perfect opening. Diving straight into the thunderclouds, he used the "Dark Terra Destroyer" he had fired earlier as cover, calculated the quickest linear trajectory, and bored into the god with a roaring "Black Tornado."

Faced with an unstoppable, drilling onslaught, Jupitermon instantly recognized he couldn't deflect it through brute force alone. To preserve his life, he made a split-second decision to sacrifice one of his arms and the heavy hammer it bore. Taking the brutal hit, he leveraged the momentum to swing his remaining hammer in a vicious arc, swatting Owl right out of the sky and slamming him into the cratered earth.

"Sacrificing a limb to save the core. Clever," Owl choked out.

"You're too kind!"

This was an exchange of respect: a warrior who had permanently lost an arm and a weapon, acknowledging the formidable might of a monstrosity whose chest armor had just been severely damaged.

My own battlefield was comparatively straightforward. My opponent was Minervamon. However, her behavior was the absolute antithesis of her wise-sounding name. She was simple to such a degree that against an adversary with zero fighting intent like myself, her only strategy was to rush forward and hack at me. The greatsword "Olympia" she brandished was terrifyingly sharp, and having absolutely no desire to be sliced in half, all I could do was constantly evade, or at worst, flick out a "Thorn Whip" to bind her.

Even bound, she refused to quiet down. Left with no other option, I channeled an electrical charge through the vines, attempting to put her to sleep on the spot—only to discover she was entirely immune to electrocution. Worse, she forcefully wrestled her greatsword free and hacked through the constricting vines, violently reclaiming her freedom.

Without a word, she launched a "Strike Roll" directly at me. While dodging, I immediately deployed "Rosy Velvet" in a desperate bid to restrict her movement on the ground. With a high-pitched, girlish battle cry, she tore herself from the ground, soaring into an explosive horizontal spin—the "Madness Merry-go-round." A colossal tornado birthed from her blades raged across the field. Such a powerful move was far beyond anything I could parry.

Praying that Owl could somehow bail me out, I threw a glance over my shoulder, only to see him locked in a grueling deathmatch under Jupitermon's relentless surveillance. He couldn't spare me a single breath. The rest of my vanguard was completely out of sight. I was totally alone. If I was going to survive, I had to save myself.

My mind raced. Suddenly, a colossal "Giant Missile" screamed down from the heavens. I guessed Dove, the [Defense Position], was fulfilling his duty once more. That meant it was time for me, the [Support Position], to pull my own weight. Without a second thought, I dropped to the dirt. Trapped within the chaotic vortex of her own tornado, Minervamon wasn't nearly so fortunate. Completely blind to the plummeting payload, she brought her greatsword up, letting the cyclone's momentum carry her strike straight into the warhead. The missile detonated on contact. The concussive blast blew her out of the air like a ragdoll, sending her skidding face-first into the mud. It was as comical as it was embarrassing. If the person she liked had witnessed a faceplant this undignified, she'd probably never get married in this life.

But this wasn't fun and games—our strict objective was to pin down all ten Mediators. We had to stall for time, buying enough leeway for whoever Owl dispatched to investigate the mastermind to get results as soon as possible. I could not let her escape.

Even if she just kissed the dirt and was now glaring daggers at you with a few humiliated, crystalline tears welling in her eyes, you couldn't afford a single drop of sympathy. In fact, empathy was fatal. The correct play was to ruthlessly mock her. Specifically, the venomous, biting mockery completely exclusive to women. The results were instantaneous. Screaming like a genuine madwoman, she lunged at me with murderous intent. This was the trap. All you had to do was relentlessly lash her with the whip, scourging her flesh until she finally breached your guard, tackling you to the ground in a frenzied rage. Only then, at point-blank range, would you wrap your arms around her, pulling her deeply into your embrace, allowing her to personally experience just how peacefully suffocating slumbering inside a "Rosy Cradle" could be.

Staring up into the churning sky, I watched jagged bolts of lightning tear through the atmosphere, briefly illuminating countless sparkling arcs from an ethereal rainbow. It was a bleak reminder of just how agonizingly difficult the battles for Guild Master Wind and the "Rainbow Starlight" duo must have been.

A sudden, deafening sonic boom shattered the air far above. Crane and Junomon plummeted from the sky as a tangled, brawling blur, crashing violently into a densely forested valley. I instinctively moved to back them up, but the moment I took a single step, the entire forest warped, generating a violent attraction magnetic field. Without a doubt, that was the result of Crane's "Chaos Degradation."

I threw my back against a massive boulder, digging my heels in just to keep from being sucked into the void. Before the dimensional rift could fully collapse, an eardrum-shattering explosion erupted just over the ridge. Peeking over the rock face, Dove's hulking silhouette smashed through my line of sight, with Owl and a highly agitated Jupitermon in hot pursuit. Factoring in the sleeping Minervamon in the dirt beside me, this was the reality of my immediate environment: tense, thrilling, yet somehow tedious.

I'll say it again: I loathe combat, but this world demands that I fight. Right when you drag yourself through a grueling deathmatch, finally allowing your lungs to heave in a deep breath of relief... another bloodbath kicks off right under your nose.

This time, the instigator was Jupitermon.

Noticing the peacefully slumbering Minervamon, he cracked his heavy hammer against the sky. A towering pillar of lightning struck down from the heavens, blasting directly into her prone body and forcefully tearing her from her sleep. More accurately, he forcibly granted her a surge of energy, triggering an evolution to a higher-stage Digimon.

Minervamon, Evolution—Mervamon.

Mature. Composed. Lethally powerful, yet undeniably intoxicating. That was the utterly terrifying aura she now radiated.

Staring at the colossal greatsword "Olympia Kai" resting lightly in her right hand, and the venomous snake-head bunker "Medullia" coiled around her left, a cold knot tied itself in my stomach. The tension was palpable; the deeper my nerves frayed, the more certain I became that I stood less than zero chance against her.

"Don't panic..."

Owl materialized just in time, dropping a few consoling words. Honestly, they didn't help much.

Continuous lightning strikes drove him away, and Jupitermon gracefully bowed out, very gentlemanly leaving space for us two girls alone, wishing for Mervamon to kill me. Naturally, I wasn't about to give him the satisfaction. These days, who didn't keep a hidden trump card or two tucked up their sleeve? Defeating her was likely out of the question, but surviving? Theoretically, that was within my grasp. After all, the Boss had left me with very explicit instructions regarding the little parting gift he threw my way: *Save this for absolute life-or-death scenarios.* In his own words, the trinket was an insurance policy meant to *preserve* life, not to blindly gamble it away in a desperate slugfest.

This was a true, brutal war of attrition.

She was a Virus-type powerhouse. I was a Data-type. I held absolutely zero elemental advantage here. This meant throwing down my final, forbidden card all came down to a single terrifying question: Could she survive the alluring, fatal embrace of my "Forbidden Temptation"?

I stared her down, throwing my most vicious threat directly into her face: "Are you ready to accept my temptation?"

She hoisted her massive blade, her eyes burning with an unshakable, predatory glint. "If you've got the spine for it... bring it on!"

I've heard plenty of battle cries, but that was single-handedly the most heroic, blood-pumping line I had ever heard cross a woman's lips. I had to admit the truth: she had successfully ignited my dormant fighting spirit. Backing down was no longer an option. I had to bare my fangs, put my true trump card on the table, and fight her until one of us was dead.
