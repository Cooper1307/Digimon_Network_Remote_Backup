# Chapter 13: Confession of the Mastermind (第十三章 幕后黑手的自述)

Fifteen years ago, at Cambridge University, I fell in love with a girl. Hindered by my status as an international student and a Jew, I didn't easily express my feelings to her. I merely watched from the sidelines, silently guarding her.

At the Christmas party toward the end of that same year, she made her relationship public. The lucky guy was a boy from the college's publicity department. The two of them had met in Midgard and developed their feelings there, which made perfect sense as to why I hadn't noticed a thing. Coincidentally, I had also registered a Midgard account at the time, but I rarely logged in, treating it merely as an alternative weekend pastime. Even though I was a full stage behind them—still stuck as a Champion-level Dobermon—I didn't feel there was any fundamental difference between us. Since our outward appearances were all masked by Digimon avatars, theoretically, there shouldn't have been any major disparity.

He was Aegiochusmon, and that girl was Angewomon. Their picture-perfect romance was universally known within the **Order of Holy Knights** at the time; practically everyone praised them as rising stars. Even their Guild Master believed that if their trajectory continued, they could easily surpass the "Red and Blue Duo" and become the defining elites of their generation. As a member of the **Nether Club**, however, I didn't possess the qualifications to enter the territory of the Order of Holy Knights. I was only fit to hide outside their guild borders every day, or stalk their footsteps into the bushes—staring, gazing, listening, thinking, pondering, smiling foolishly, envying, and seething with jealousy... right up until the day they evolved to the Mega level and, in front of their entire guild, announced their engagement.

I was consumed by sheer fury, yet utterly helpless. Who was to blame but myself for lacking the power to stand against him?

I chose to swallow my pride and endure the humiliation, but in my heart, I never let him off the hook. Nursing a chest full of suffocating resentment, I planted the seeds of revenge. All it needed now was time to germinate. All I had to do was quietly wait for it to bear fruit.

During that period, I don't know what possessed me, but I actually took the initiative to approach them, staging an entrance under the guise of an absolute stranger. Thanks to the protective anonymity of our Digimon skins, there was no way they could have guessed who I was. Gradually, we became well-acquainted. The feeling was akin to getting to know them all over again.

From their own mouths, I learned about the existence of the twelve Mediators. Thanks to him, I consequently realized that exacting my revenge in the future wouldn't be so simple—unless I struck first and eliminated the other ten members.

Thus, I embarked on the path to seek true power.

Through relentless effort, constantly plying information from various contacts, and even going so far as to leverage the Nether Club's underground trafficking network, I finally arrived at that desolate wasteland situated in the Digital World. There, I obtained that hard-won, otherworldly power of darkness. It forced me to completely bypass the Ultimate stage of Cerberumon, leapfrogging my evolution straight into the Mega-level Plutomon. It granted me the strength and the nerve to rival all twelve of them combined.

Upon my return, I didn't let the power make me arrogant. Instead, I operated with meticulous caution.

The political environment in Midgard at the time was highly unfavorable for the Mediation Organization. Major guilds were intensely hostile toward one another. The Mediators usually ran themselves ragged trying to settle disputes between these factions, only to frequently find themselves the target of coordinated sieges from both warring sides. As for the reason—it was likely because the vibe they gave off to the citizens of Midgard was akin to the development team's lapdogs. They refused to listen to either side's justifications, entirely disregarding the actual truth of the matter. They strictly operated according to their so-called "principles." If someone refused to listen, after a few perfunctory warnings, they would resort to violence. Their behavior was indistinguishable from ruthless law enforcement agents in the real world.

In Midgard, everyone yearned for freedom. Everyone desired to live without restraints. The vast majority of users chose to log in here precisely to escape the suffocating restrictions of reality, and the very existence of the Mediation Organization had ironically become a massive obstacle. How could people like them, an organization like theirs, possibly be welcomed by the masses?

The situation was overwhelmingly in my favor. Adhering to the principle of striking them down one by one and dismantling them individually, I conducted a thorough reconnaissance on the respective combat prowess of all twelve members. I decided to start by picking off the softest target among them. For instance, the youngest—Venusmon.

Her innocence honestly made me envious, and the sheer speed at which she walked step-by-step into my trap was staggering. I didn't even have to bait her, which saved me a considerable amount of effort. I conjured a patch of Chaos out of thin air to trap her, then tore open "Hell's Gate," intending to suck her into the void and erase all traces of her existence. Who could have anticipated that a "Kikurin" blade would suddenly slash across my vision, cleaving through the conceptual Chaos and leaving a deep, burning trench across my chest, severely disrupting my master plan.

I was furious beyond belief. Lunging forward, I engaged the owner of the "Kikurin" in a desperate fight to the death.

Back then, I was arrogant and brimming with youthful bravado. I had no idea what kind of legendary figure the "Ba-ye" standing before me actually was, so naturally, I was beaten within an inch of my life. Enduring this crushing defeat forced me to thoroughly comprehend a pivotal truth: you cannot choose to fight alone simply because your target for revenge is a single individual. At the critical moment, unforeseen variables will always step in to play savior. The optimal strategy is "murder with a borrowed knife." That way, at the very least, you won't be stretched too thin, allowing you to focus one hundred percent of your cognitive capacity on the target of your vengeance.

Soon after Midgard entered its official operational phase, I finally grasped such an opportunity.

To say I found the opportunity would be inaccurate; it's more fitting to say the opportunity found me. On that particular day, the Guild Master of the Wind-Chasing Brigade—Wind—painted her face in heavy makeup and marched into the Nether Club's territory. She tracked down our Guild Master, Jack (Knave), and demanded to see me.

"Are you sure it's her?"

"Aside from me, she's the only person in all of Midgard who could pull off a feral makeup job like that."

I had to admire Jack's brutal honesty. If he didn't paint himself up like a clown every single day, he'd be physically uncomfortable.

The moment we met, the feral Guild Master Wind cut straight to the chase: she asked if I wanted to exact revenge on the Mediation Organization. Upon hearing this, Jack and I exchanged a knowing smirk. Jack then turned his face, sucked in a sharp breath of cold air, and, plastering on a wide grin, dropped a casual warning—"The Nether Club does not foot the bill for personal vendettas"—before voluntarily excusing himself from our conversation.

After he left, I locked eyes with Wind and gave her a single-word answer: Yes.

"Are you familiar with the 'Free Birds'?"

"A civilian squad of nosy Raiders. I've heard the rumors."

"What do you think of them?"

"What do I think of them?" I was slightly perplexed. "What exactly are you trying to imply? Pitting the five of them against the twelve Mediators—how is that any different from sending them to their deaths?"

She informed me that it wouldn't just be them. She would personally take the field, dragging along "Rainbow Starlight"—the defining elites of the Wind-Chasing Brigade—and that her "old neighbor" would also lend a hand.

"Are you absolutely certain Ultra has the spare time for this?"

"He's never once complained about my requests. Besides, the target this time is the Mediation Organization—the very people he's wanted to tear down countless times in the past. You of all people must know just how many operations those twelve ruined for us back in the day..."

"Stop right there." I held up a hand to cut her off. "I'm not exactly a Guild Master. How the hell am I supposed to know how much bad blood you have with them? Furthermore, I honestly don't care to know. If you have that much breath to waste, you'd be better off detailing the actionable strategy. Let me hear if the words spilling out of a high-flying bird's beak are actually feasible."

She granted my wish, pivoting to reintroduce herself: "Aurora, civilian Master of Hypnosis."

She deliberately used the word "Master," indicating absolute, unshakable confidence in her abilities in this field. I, however, remained highly skeptical, stating that unless she was willing to perform a live demonstration right in front of my eyes, it would be extremely difficult to convince me.

"There's no need for that." She smiled at me. "You're already under my hypnosis. Have you truly not figured it out yet?"

I instinctively tensed up, demanding, "Prove it."

"The fact that your Guild Master hasn't murdered me yet—that is the very best proof."

Fair point.

During the open beta, guilds operated as isolated factions engaged in endless brutal warfare. Jack had personally stated that Wind was *his* prey. Even though Midgard's political climate had tended toward peace for years, the deep-seated hatred between the major Guild Masters wouldn't just evaporate so easily. Cordially hosting an arch-rival in one's own territory was a literal impossibility.

"So, you're saying you simultaneously hypnotized both of us?"

"Not at all. I only hypnotized you." She clarified, "I implanted a subconscious suggestion, forcing you to believe that the man who escorted me in a moment ago was your Guild Master. And you believed it without a shadow of a doubt, didn't you?"

I couldn't argue with her logic, but I also didn't trust her in the slightest. I could only play along. "Let's just assume that's the case."

Following that, she produced a photograph of a man and slid it across the table. She told me this person was the leader of the "Free Birds." She intended to subject him to psychological suggestion, manipulating him into helping us achieve our objectives.

"Even if someone has to take the fall, it'll be him. Who told him to be the one to voice the strategy out loud?"

I roughly grasped her intent. Honestly, the tactic was utterly despicable. First, orchestrate a theatrical standoff with Ultra to manufacture the illusion that their two major guilds were locked in a blood feud. Then, dispatch a "messenger" who had long-established ties with the "Free Birds" to feed them the bait, drawing them in, securing them as our vanguard, and placing them in open, violent opposition to the Mediation Organization. It was pure, unadulterated madness.

"And what if you fail to overpower the ten Mediators...?"

"Then it will be your turn to take the stage."

She was implying I should handle the dirty work of plunging a knife into their backs—which aligned perfectly with my own intentions.

It was blatantly obvious she was using me. Using me as the final executioner to swoop in at the eleventh hour and harvest the heads. If the operation was a success, everyone walked away happy. If it failed, the guillotine would drop entirely on my neck. I would be branded public enemy number one, universally reviled as the villain. Even if I blew the whistle and named her and Ultra as the true masterminds, not a single soul would believe me. I had to admit, her calculated scheming was brilliant—it was just a pity she tried to play her hand on the wrong person. Consequently, I decided to seize this exact opportunity to exact a brutal, fitting revenge against her.

I refused to be the apex predator merely cleaning up a squabble between bottom-feeders. I was going to be the fisherman who dragged the entire net ashore without expending an ounce of real effort, walking away with the ultimate prize. To achieve that, I needed a genuinely irresistible bait.

And so, the trap was set.

Ten days later, I received an extremely unorthodox package mailed from Ukraine. Inside was a mummified, severed head. Judging by the remaining hair length, it belonged to a woman. Accompanying it was a letter penned by one of Jack's subordinates—the leader of a local mercenary outfit. The letter explicitly stated that the original owner of the head was the civilian hypnotist named Aurora. In other words, the legendary Guild Master Wind. Taped to the bottom of the missive was her encrypted Identity Verification Card. How I played the board from this point onward was entirely up to my own discretion.

I freely admit, the situation escalating to this extreme was completely beyond my wildest expectations.

If the objective was revenge, or even terminating a human life—that could easily be accomplished within the Digital World without leaving a single trace of forensic evidence. Yet Jack deliberately chose to orchestrate a physical hit in the real world. I couldn't fathom his reasoning. Based on my understanding of Jack, even if I logged into Midgard, jammed a gun to his head, and interrogated him face-to-face, he would simply feign absolute ignorance. He'd insist it was the unsanctioned, rogue behavior of a subordinate, and since he firmly held the stance that he 'does not foot the bill for personal vendettas,' he naturally couldn't provide a reasonable explanation. At most, he’d offer some nauseating humanitarian platitudes, hypocritically mourning the loss for a few sentences, fully intending to disgust you so thoroughly with his facade that you'd abandon the questioning altogether. From start to finish, you'd never actually decipher what endgame he was calculating.

Scrawled across the back of the letter was a single, chilling sentence: *You're welcome.*

His methodology was masterfully terrifying—it was functionally the equivalent of carving "I did this" onto the victim's forehead, yet remaining politically untouchable. An airtight plan, flawless timing, and a ruthless executor. With all three conditions perfectly satisfied, he had orchestrated an immaculate vengeance.

Having acquired Wind's account, I logged in and initiated contact with Ultra. He didn't harbor a single shadow of doubt regarding my identity, treating me with the same warmth reserved for his old neighbor. The conspiracy advanced flawlessly, step by step. It was vividly clear that Wind had successfully implanted the post-hypnotic suggestion into Owl right before her execution. Or, more accurately, Jack had dispatched his hit squad to sever Wind's head the very second she finished implanting the suggestion in Owl. The mantis stalks the cicada, utterly blind to the oriole looming behind. But in my scenario, as the oriole, I was physically forced to wear the skin of the devoured cicada. What was far worse was the fact that the predator locking onto me was Ceresmon—who, identical to the avatar I was currently piloting, was an absolutely massive avian.

Fortunately, I had thoroughly researched my opponent's fatal flank in advance: her true core, the humanoid "Ceresmon Medium" mounted atop her head. She possessed a terrifyingly lethal skill designated "Hunger," which allowed her to manipulate parasitic flora to siphon an enemy's life-force until their data was entirely hollowed out. The Ornismon avatar I was operating disastrously fell victim to this exact execution. Not only was the devastating "Cosmic Rays" I fired completely absorbed, but the entire digital superstructure of the beast rapidly fragmented into loose data chunks, slowly dissolving into the ether until nothing remained.

However, because we were firmly entrenched within the localized boundaries of Midgard rather than the untethered expanses of the external Digital World, localized avatar death did not equate to absolute deletion. To me, right now, it simply signaled the cue for my grand entrance.

Forcibly ejected back into reality, I retrieved my authentic Identity Verification Card, slammed it into the rig, and re-initialized the dive. I crashed back onto the battlefield manifesting as Plutomon, effortlessly decapitating Ceresmon with a single, brutal cast of "Haggard Cluster."

The massacre continued to escalate.

Holding Dianamon hostage to cripple Apollomon seamlessly prevented the apocalyptic threat of their fusion evolution into GraceNovamon. With them removed from the board, the remaining two little girls comprising "Rainbow Starlight" were naturally no match for me. As we clashed violently in the upper stratosphere, absolutely no one on the ground had any line of sight on the slaughter occurring above the cloud banks. I resorted to a minor, dirty trick. Tearing open a "Hell's Gate," I permanently exiled Hououmon alongside the devastating inferno of her "Starlight Explosion." Simultaneously, I force-hacked a segment of AncientIrismon's base code, critically short-circuiting the "User Identification Medal" embedded in her core. The catastrophic failure forcefully reverted her into a mindless, native Digimon, sending her plummeting from the heavens. Predictably, her death spiral painted a gorgeously ironic rainbow across the sky.

With the sky cleared, I descended to pay a visit to my former sweetheart. She was, after all, the crowning jewel of my revenge. At that exact moment, she and Mastemon were locked in a desperate death-grapple, viciously strangling each other. Identifying the perfect opening, I struck them from behind. By weaponizing a localized field of Chaos to mask my trajectory, I manipulated the combat data to make them both believe the other had landed a critical blow. Afterward, I retreated comfortably into the cloud layer, soaking in the serene satisfaction of watching their paralyzed bodies plummet toward the earth.

Touching down at the impact crater, I found both women severely concussed from the freefall, yet still rabidly attempting to shred each other entirely on feral instinct. I stepped between them, forcefully breaking their grapple, and delivered a brutal backhand to each of their faces. Instead of shocking them into clarity, the violence only triggered them to behave even more hysterically, like screeching harpies.

Mastemon was the first to redirect her bloodlust toward me. In rapid succession, she fired off her two most vaunted signature executions—"Holy Desire" and "Dark Despair"—both of which I effortlessly swatted aside. Realizing she couldn't secure a rapid victory, she completely abandoned tactical restraint and slammed down her ultimate trump card: "Chaos Degradation." Ironically, the resulting dimensional rift didn't damage me in the slightest. Conversely, it afforded me the perfect window of slowed time to wrap my arms around my former beloved, forcefully injecting my core virus directly into her code. I gifted her the exact same otherworldly dark power I possessed, forcing her system into a catastrophic breakthrough. Right before my eyes, she warped into Junomon: Hysteric Mode.

In this corrupted state, her digital signature would be utterly imperceptible to Jupitermon. In other words, I could weaponize her perfectly.

As expected of the woman I had once given my heart to; she didn't disappoint. Wielding the venomous stingers on her gauntlets, she effortlessly shredded Mastemon's holy armor, impaling the angel's torso and forcing her into a paralyzing state of suspended animation. Raising Mastemon's broken body high above her head like a grisly hunting trophy, she dragged the angel for a considerable distance. Only after sweeping the environment and confirming the presence of fresh prey did she discard the bleeding angel into the dirt like worthless garbage.

"Such a temperamental girl," I mused aloud, casually scanning our surroundings. Yet, my radar remained completely empty. The chilling realization that *I* was the newly designated prey hit me a fraction of a second too late. She had already coiled her barbed chains around my throat, fully prepared to impale me with the same vicious apathy she had shown the garbage she just discarded.

Honestly, in that agonizing microsecond, my chest physically ached. It was as if I had been violently dragged back to the exact year she had shattered my heart. I wanted to weep, but the tears buried within my soul had long since bled dry, leaving nothing behind but a boiling cavity of seething, hot blood.

To ensure my own survival, I had no alternative. I channeled a searing "Light of Chaos" and executed her on the spot—simultaneously executing the last lingering fragments of my love from all those years ago.

The moment I watched Jupitermon psychologically break and collapse over her corpse, the true weight of my obsession finally dawned on me. Over all these years, the true focal point of my hatred had subtly drifted from the girl who rejected me to the man standing faithfully by her side. Completely blinded by the intoxicating promise of revenge, I never realized that the space he occupied within my mind had grown to vastly outstrip my beloved girl. This was, without a shadow of a doubt, a tragedy.

An absolute, irredeemable tragedy, meticulously orchestrated by my own hands from beginning to end.
