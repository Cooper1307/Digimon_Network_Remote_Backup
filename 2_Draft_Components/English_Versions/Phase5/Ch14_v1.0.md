# Chapter 14: Dragon and God

Both were users who settled into **Midgard** during the closed beta period, both were pioneers who were active on the front lines during the open beta period. One was recruited by the later Raiders, one was selected by the former development team. One created Free Birds, one formed the Mediation Organization.

One was named Owl, one was named Vulture.

They were the same—both were strong, both became heroes, and both harvested their respective feelings afterward.

They were also different. One left the guild because of a bet and gradually faded from the world of **Midgard**, with no newcomers knowing who he was; the other became a target of public ire among guilds back then—anyone at the Mega level didn't take him seriously.

Now they meet again, both following their own path, each calling the other their enemy, neither mentioning the time they fought side by side back then, as if they didn't know each other from the beginning. Everyone knew it was meaningless to continue fighting—it was thankless and would lead to injuries—yet no one was willing to back off. The reason was obvious: the opponent's existence was enough to constitute their reason to continue fighting.

Between the exchanges, the hearts of both were shouting to the other: stop fighting, or die.

The hearts of both were struggling. Neither wanted to hurt the other, nor did they want to be hurt by the other, and even less did they want to see their own people harmed by the other's. They were at the mercy of circumstance: explosions echoed across the sky, thunderbolts struck down with ferocity. Time and again, they unleashed their signature moves on each other, watching as the other was hit and disappeared into the hazy smoke. Neither slackened, for they knew each other well—it wouldn't be long before the other retaliated in kind. This was the pact they had made together back then.

"In the future, if you and I become enemies, please don't take it easy on me. Treat me as you should, as if we don't know each other, so as not to affect each other's judgment."
"You too. I don't want to be a sentimental fellow. I also want to lose somewhat decently. So, in case there really is such a day, do not be polite with me where you should take my life. Being defeated by the **Jet-Black Hero** is my honor."

Facing **Mabolt** with **War Blaster**, the Dragon Warrior felt no confusion.
Facing **Dramon Killers** with a pair of **Judgment Hammers** in his hands, the God of the Sky made no compromise.

Dragon and God, holding respect for each other, harboring hostility, understanding each other's feelings, and at the same time empathizing with the tactics used, pondering each other's thoughts, continuously testing, and reacting timely to eliminate future troubles.

Heavy hammers struck the **Dramon Killers**, splashing dancing sparks and leaving scorched marks for the judgment current descending from the sky to track. The Dragon Warrior raised both arms' **Dramon Killers**, merged them together, rotated his body at high speed, dispersed the **Punish Judge** from the God of the Sky, and at the same time caused the air around him to start rotating, gradually gathering to form a **Black Tornado** with a wide range, returning it as a gift of many years to his old friend.

In a flash of gazes, it was as if returning to those years.

"You have no sword, not even a decent weapon, yet you come to learn swordsmanship from Master Muca. What for? Can you talk to me, Owl?"
"To be able to use it one day."
"Nonsense."
"And you?" the Dragon Warrior asked back.
"The same as you," the God of the Sky snorted and replied: "I need power and technique far beyond yours to prevent myself from losing to a strong person like you in the future. If you think I'm being modest or flattering, then I might have to disappoint you."

Dragon took God as a competitor; he saw the "responsibility" the other bore on his shoulders. God took Dragon as his goal; he saw the "courage" stored in the other's heart. They were both the truly strong in each other's eyes, both respected each other, and at the same time, both looked down on each other, just because the other wasn't on the same path.

Between "Truth" and "Principle," the Dragon Warrior chose the former, while the God of the Sky chose the latter.

Both raised their swords simultaneously. One severed the shackles called "Principle" in exchange for the freedom to pursue "Truth"; the other split open the confusion brought by "Truth" and held onto the "Principle" he revered in his heart.

No one was right, and no one was wrong.

"What about you, Master Muca?"

Both wanted to know. However, times had changed, and the person who could answer this question had forever bid farewell to them. They would never get a satisfactory answer—or rather, they had already gotten that satisfactory answer.

Pursue "Truth" without losing "Principle."

Actually, their teacher had long set an example and answered them with practical actions. Until today—to be precise, after they were hit by the ultimate moves released by the other—they only then realized: when the fully charged **Dark Terra Destroyer** collided head-on with **Wide-Range Divine Punishment**, the two violent impacts clashed, repelled each other, and finally merged completely to be released outward—the generated energy was enough to shake the entirety of **Midgard**.

Excellent warriors always keep a trick up their sleeve, and neither of them was an exception. One unstrapped the shield from his back, the other drew the feather cloak behind him, to resist the impact they suffered.

In the process, the God of the Sky, Vulture, suddenly sensed the fall of his wife, Junomon. He was extremely furious, voiced a roar at the sky, and then turned grief and indignation into strength, swallowed the **Seed of Promotion** inherited from his wife, cast aside the feather cloak blocking him, and began to face that destructive damage head-on.

"Lagi, I will live well for your share... For that, I must not fall here!"

At that moment, he broke through the limit, continuously gathering the energy brought by the impact around him until, at last, it reshaped his very body. The golden color deepened, and the **Judgment Hammers** transformed into a great lightning sword, thus completing a qualitative leap.

**Jupitermon**
**Mode Change**
**Jupitermon: Wrath Mode**

"Is it time?"

Owl looked at Vulture before him, inexplicably showing a few traces of sentimentality.

He was so unwilling to face such a painful him, but if he didn't, his own people would have to, and among his own people, no one was his match. Therefore, Owl must make a choice before Vulture could act.

He once reached the end of the **Guild War**, only one step away from winning, but unfortunately lost to the person he made a bet with and lost the qualification to obtain the **Seed of Promotion**. After that, by chance, the two met again. Thinking the world was small, less meeting would be better, but as it turned out, the Dragon gave the **Seed of Promotion** he had obtained to Owl, which made Owl very confused and unable to understand.

"Such humiliation, I'm afraid I cannot accept it."
"What are you thinking!" the Dragon was indifferent.
"I don't know the winner's intention."

Without much nonsense with Owl, the Dragon stated bluntly: "A guy like me with an undefeated body doesn't need this stuff. On the contrary, look at the people following you all day, and you're so eager to get it—I'll help you a small favor. Just use it to protect the people around you. Don't wait until you lose them for regret to set in..."

He was right; he truly needed such an opportunity, right here and now.

"Falcon, Dove, Crane, Sakura—for you, I... must win this battle!"

Swallowing the **Seed of Promotion**, Owl decided to go all out in the battle.

A huge globe of light instantly engulfed Owl, peeling off a portion of the violent impact and merging it into his body, causing the **Dramon Killers** on both his arms to gradually transform into a massive sword named **Dramon Breaker**. The **Brave Shield** on his back began to contract inward, becoming more suitable for offense. Last of all, his body changed from its original black to orange.

**BlackWarGreymon**
**Species Change**
**VictoryGreymon**

Breaking through the limit, sword against sword—this was the best way to commemorate his master.

"Thanks, Dragon."

The combat time limit was only a short ten minutes. Exceeding the limit meant defeat. If they wanted to decide the outcome within the limit, both had to apply 120% of their focus to respond; neither could hold back.

Through the organization's internal voice system, Vulture notified the remaining of his people, telling them to stop as soon as possible, and also telling them to tell their respective opponents to give up the meaningless struggle. The win or loss now depended on the final showdown between the two captains; all hope was pinned on them.

"This day, I've waited for a long time..."
"Who hasn't!"

The heroic Dragon Warrior took the massive sword from his shoulder and pointed it at his old friend, taking the lead in stating his position: "**Data Deletion Style**, Owl—entering the stage!"
The God of the Sky followed closely: "**Data Deletion Style**, Vulture—entering the stage!"

Afterward, both simultaneously showed a confident tone toward each other, gripped the massive swords in their hands at the same time, and quickly approached each other after the impact before them completely dissipated. Two massive swords crossed, parried each other, producing massive energy that pushed the two apart, forcing them to pull distance again.

The head-on clash yielded no results, so both immediately changed strategy, launching a series of sword strikes supported by speed. Dragging the massive swords in their hands, the two shuttled between the forest and the wilderness. Every swing was exceptionally heavy, and the damage caused was enough to reshape the terrain. Both being disciples of Muca's Data Deletion Style, both worked equally hard and both received the master's personal instruction. Therefore, in terms of swordsmanship, the two were evenly matched, making it nearly impossible to decide a winner.

Owl swung his sword, and Vulture retreated and jumped onto a nearby tree, then raised the lightning sword in his hand and fired a golden flash of lightning to the sky. A large patch of thunderclouds responded to his call, raining down fierce thunderbolts in response.

In the face of the lightning's glare, Owl’s evasion seemed as slow as an aging tortoise. Unwilling to be decapitated, he could only stand his ground, swinging his massive sword again and again to rebuff the "invitations from heaven."

Seeing this, Vulture rallied once more, hovering in the sky. He moved into Owl's blind spot, infused a portion of his energy into the lightning sword, and then plunged it down toward Owl—executing a new form of **Punish Judge**. Owl, however, had long since noticed his old friend’s "treacherous" intent and kept a sharp eye on his movements. As the lightning sword struck down from behind his head, Owl immediately gripped his **Dramon Breaker** and countered with **Victory Charge**, using the massive blade as a shield to absorb the energy stored in the enemy's sword before releasing it back—making it look as though Vulture's own attack had been perfectly reflected.

Caught off guard by Owl's maneuver, Vulture suffered the sting of his own power. Before he could recover his stability in the air, Owl's next retaliation was already upon him.

The ten-minute limit was fast approaching; time was running out.

Without a second thought, Owl unleashed his ultimate move: **Trident Gaia**. He spun the hilt, detaching the central ridge of the sword and drawing it out, then folded the cross-guard pieces together so the left and right blades connected at the pommel, forming a new, specialized blade. Reaching for a dual-wielding style, he concentrated all the atmospheric energy into the tips of the two edges, gradually forming a colossal energy sphere.

Seeing his old friend intended to end the battle quickly, Vulture felt the same. He transformed his very being into high-pressure plasma, infusing it into the lightning sword. Just as Owl was about to hurl the massive energy sphere, Vulture reversed the blade and drove the lightning sword into the ground with all his might, releasing the most destructive form of **Wide-Range Divine Punishment**.

**Midgard** was shaken once again as the two extreme forces collided.

"Guild Master—"

"Don't go out there!"

Adam timely barked a warning to Queen, who was about to head out and investigate. He simultaneously stopped the excited behavior of the patrons in the bar, telling them to stay calm. "Listen, everyone: the Mediation Organization is just performing their duties. We’re just a small guild; we can't afford to provoke them. Curiosity killed the cat."

As he spoke, he looked back and gave the bartender a meaningful glance.

"If you ask me, folks, we're all sensible people here. We’d best just sit back down and have a drink. The next round is on the house—drink as much as you like, don't be shy!"

If one looked closely, they would notice that President Adam's back was already soaked in cold sweat. Even without being on-site, he could feel the sheer scale of the event, and he couldn't help but feel a twinge of worry for the Free Birds.

Deep within the explosion where their combined energies converged, the two warriors pressed their swords against each other. Their convictions had reached their peak; there was no room for hesitation or doubt. Each desired only to defeat the formidable opponent before them and reach the next summit.

"Are you prepared to fall, Owl?!"

"It's far too early for that!"

After the brief exchange, both simultaneously unleashed the decisive techniques of the Data Deletion Style. A cold flash of steel sliced through the air, and the battle between Dragon and God finally reached its end.

The smoke dissipated, and the setting sun cast its glow. A wisp of a breeze brushed past their ears. In this moment, they both collapsed to the ground, the will to fight gone as they reverted to their human forms.

"Tell me, Owl... why did I lose?"

"You didn't."

"Bullshit! A loss is a loss; I'm not the type to deny it. So you'd best not try to appease me with that hypocrite's face of yours—I’m not buying it."

Hearing this, Owl finally felt forced to deliver the heart-stabbing truth: "You never received Master Muca's true legacy."

"Legacy? I didn't even know such a thing existed... What is it?"

"The final secret technique of the **Data Deletion Style**, one that can only be performed with dual blades—the **Rakukyoukijuzan (Fallen Owl, Returning Vulture Slash)**. Listen to that strange name... doesn't it describe exactly who we are?"

"Heh... I suppose it does."

With that, Vulture’s eyes showed a sense of resignation. Gazing up at the sky that didn’t feel so distant now, he let out a sigh of realization. "We’re still the same as ever... we never truly understood him. We really were beaten by him, weren't we?"

It felt like a mix of nostalgia for the past and gratitude toward their mentor.

Master Muca... you truly were the greatest blacksmith in all of **Midgard**. Knowing you had to bid farewell to this world, you didn't forget to forge two swords that were every bit the equal of the **Kikurin**... One named 'Owl,' and one named 'Vulture.'

Thanks to you, those two swords are still as sharp as ever today. Just like you were back then, they watch over **Midgard** at every moment.

"Our people will be here soon."

"Who hasn't got people coming? Everyone goes home, everyone finds their own way. Today’s business is over; we'll act like none of it ever happened."

"Why don't we call them all together and go for a drink?"

"Your treat."

"I'll kill you."
